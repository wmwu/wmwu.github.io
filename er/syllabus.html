The Course

PHIL 18 is an introductory course on Eastern Religions. It aims to help students gain a good understanding of major Eastern religions and broaden their worldviews. We will study Hinduism, Buddhism, Yin Yang, Confucianism and Daoism. We will learn how the theological, mythological, ritual, ethical, social and experiential dimensions of religion intertwine with each other to form different worldviews and ways of life. Students will also learn how to compare and evaluate various religious teachings and see how they may affect one’s life.

Recommended Preparation Reading Level 5, English Level 4
Transfer Status UC 3B, CSU C2
Course Format Lecture and Discussion
Objectives/SLO Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
  1. Recognize and distinguish between various world views of the major Eastern religions.
  2. Analyze and evaluate complex elements of religious phenomena.
  3. Compare, appraise and evaluate the interrelationships between particular cultures and religious expressions.
  4. Assess and value diversity of Eastern religions.
  5. Analyze and explain how ideas and practice from various Eastern religions address important questions in life, and assess their applicability to the human condition.

Assignments and Assessments

Reading Assignments

Philosophical texts can be hard to read and difficult to understand. It is quite common that one needs to read the text several times to fully grasp its main points. To help yourself gain a better understanding of a topic and become better prepared to participate in discussions and classroom activities, it is paramount that you read the assigned materials before the class on the date they are due.

  • Read a chapter or an article as soon as you can after it is assigned or recommended.
  • Outline what you read, take notes and jot down questions.
  • Do online search to learn more about new terms and philosophical views.
  • Reread the chapter or the article at least one more time after the lecture and class discussion to make sure you understand the finer points. Revise and update your notes, and use them for writing assignments.
  • Your reading comprehension will be evaluated via outlines and essay questions on Canvas. There is no make-up for missed online submissions. No late submission, resubmission or paper copy will be accepted. If you miss an assignment, you are encouraged to make up the lost points through extra-credit.

Writing Assignments

The amount of writing will exceed the 1,500-word requirement. The types of writing include outlines of reading materials, essay questions and forum discussions. All the writings are to be submitted on Canvas and are graded on both form and content. There is no make-up, and no late submission, resubmission or paper copy will be accepted.

Reading Outlines

To assess your reading comprehension, you will be asked to outline reading materials and post them on Canvas. This is one way you can learn from each other and contribute to the quality and learning success of the class.

Essay Questions

Essay questions are used to assess your comprehension and philosophical thinking.

Canvas Discussions

We will use Canvas Discussions to foster open and in-depth discussions of philosophical and religious issues. It’s a great way to apply critical thinking, share viewpoints and learn from one another.

Tests

There will be three in-class tests: one on Hinduism, one on Buddhism and one on the Chinese Worldview. You should use the reading outlines as study guides to study for the tests. The test format includes True-False and Multiple Choice. Each test is worth 100-150 points. Students who miss a test with legitimate reasons can make it up for full or partial credit.

Grading

It is important that we study for self-improvement and self-fulfillment, and not just for grades. Your grade is to help you gauge what you have learned in a course. By letting go of the anxiety about the grade, you will find studying a subject matter much more enjoyable. As a result, you are more likely to spend time learning the material and earn a good grade. Only a hard-earned grade that truly reflects what you have learned can forge self-confidence.

Your final grade will be determined by the percentage of the total points you earn:

Open, Safe and Respectful Learning Environment

This is our class and it is important that we work together to establish an open, safe and respectful learning environment. We pledge to respect all people regardless of sexuality, gender identity, ethnicity and religious backgrounds.

The most important guideline to remember is the Golden Rule—Do not do to the others what you do not want done to yourself. As you are respectful to your teacher and classmates, they will treat you in the same way. Please comply with the following etiquette:

  • Always conduct yourself in an appropriate and respectable manner.
  • Attend to personal needs before coming to class.
  • Come to class on time and avoid leaving early or in the middle of a session.
  • Respect other people even if you disagree with their views.
  • Wait for your turn to ask questions or express your viewpoints; do not chitchat in the classroom.
  • No food, drink or chewing gum.

Tips for Success

To be successful in the class and earn the grade you desire, you need to manage your time wisely and put in your effort.

  • Try your best not to miss classes.
  • Read the reading assignment and give the subject some thought before the discussion in class and online.
  • Review lecture notes as soon as possible after class.
  • Ask questions if something is unclear to you.
  • Reflect upon the issues or questions as often as possible.
  • Draft your answers to essay questions and revise them before submitting them.
  • Don’t wait until the last minute to complete online assignments.

Getting Help

The best way to get help is to ask questions in class. People shy away from asking questions because they are afraid of appearing stupid. It is very likely that if you do not understand something, there is someone else in the class who is suffering from an identical lack of understanding. You will be doing your classmates a service by asking questions.

If something is unclear to you or you are having difficulty learning the material, please feel free to come to see me during my office hours. If you cannot meet with me during the regular hours, I will be glad to schedule an appointment. Do not wait until it is too late.
