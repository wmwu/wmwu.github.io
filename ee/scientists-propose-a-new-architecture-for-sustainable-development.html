Scientists Propose a New Architecture for Sustainable Development

Over the last several decades, sustainable human development has been conceived largely as the outcome of balanced work on three “pillars” — economic and social development and environmental protection. As a United Nations working group negotiates a set of “sustainable development goals,” 10 scientists and development analysts have proposed a fundamentally different way to frame this concept.
