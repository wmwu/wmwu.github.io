The Course

PHIL 5 is an introductory course on theories of environmental ethics and how they are applied to sustainability and environmental issues. Both traditional and contemporary, Western and non-Western theories are studied to learn various outlooks on how humans should live in nature, including how we relate to the environment, the future generations and other species. The ideas and principles are then used to find solutions to the challenges we face today such as resource depletion, pollution, climate change and species extinction.

Recommended Preparation Reading Level 5, English Level 4, Eligibility for English 2, Introduction to Logic
Transfer Status UC, CSU
Course Format Lecture and Discussion
Objectives/SLO Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
  1. Explain and articulate the major environmental ethical theories
  2. Summarize and critique arguments for and against various theories
  3. Develop and formulate personal outlook by synthesizing various perspectives
  4. Identify the major problems in environmental ethics and explore solutions
  5. Relate environmental ethics to other fields such as ecology, sociology and the development of new technologies
  6. Apply the theories to resolve contemporary environmental issues

Assignments and Assessments

Reading Assignments

The textbook is to provide basic information of the subject matter. Additional materials and readings are provided on Canvas.

Philosophical texts can be hard to read and difficult to understand. It is quite common that one needs to read the text several times to fully grasp its main points. To help yourself gain a better understanding of a topic and become better prepared to participate in the discussion, it is paramount that you read the assigned materials before the topic is discussed in class and online.

  • Read a chapter or an article as soon as you can after it is assigned or recommended.
  • Outline what you read, take notes and jot down questions.
  • Do online search to learn more about new terms and philosophical views.
  • Use questions for reflection and discussion to make sure that you have grasped the key points.
  • Reread the chapter or the article at least one more time after the lecture and class discussion to make sure you understand the finer points. Revise and update your notes, and use them for writing assignments.
  • Your reading comprehension will be evaluated via essay questions on Canvas. There is no make-up for missed online submissions. No paper copies will be accepted; neither will late submissions or resubmissions. If you miss an assignment, you are encouraged to make up the lost points through extra-credit.

Writing Assignments

The amount of writing will exceed the 1,500-word requirement. The types of writing include essay questions, forum discussions and a reflection paper. All the writings are to be submitted on Canvas and are graded on both form and content. There is no make-up. No paper copies will be accepted; neither will late submissions or resubmissions.

Essay Questions

Essay questions are used to assess your comprehension and philosophical thinking.

Canvas Discussions

We will use Canvas Discussions to foster open and in-depth discussions of philosophical and environmental issues. It’s a great way to apply critical thinking, share viewpoints and learn from one another.

Reflection Paper

By the end of the semester, you are to turn in a reflection paper on what you have learned in the semester and how they have shaped your environmental philosophy. Keeping a journal of your thoughts on various topics throughout the semester is a good way to prepare the paper.

Grading

It is important that we study for self-improvement and self-fulfillment, and not just for grades. Your grade is to help you gauge what you have learned in a course. By letting go of the anxiety about the grade, you will find studying a subject matter much more enjoyable. As a result, you are more likely to spend time learning the material and earn a good grade. Only a hard-earned grade that truly reflects what you have learned can forge self-confidence.

Your final grade will be determined by the percentage of the total points you earn:

This is our class and it is important that we work together to establish an open, safe and respectful learning environment. We pledge to respect all people regardless of sexuality, gender identity, ethnicity and religious backgrounds.

The most important guideline to remember is the Golden Rule—Do not do to the others what you do not want done to yourself. As you are respectful to your teacher and classmates, they will treat you in the same way. Please comply with the following etiquette:

  • Attend to personal needs before coming to class.

Tips for Success

To be successful in the class and earn the grade you desire, you need to manage your time wisely and put in your effort.

  • Try your best not to miss classes.
  • Read the reading assignment and give the subject some thought before the discussion in class and online.
  • Review lecture notes as soon as possible after class.
  • Ask questions if something is unclear to you.
  • Reflect upon the issues or questions as often as possible.
  • Draft your answers to essay questions and revise them before submitting them.
  • Don’t wait until the last minute to complete online assignments.
